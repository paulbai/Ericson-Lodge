- **From Freetown:** Approximately 4–5 hours by road (~300 km). Bush taxis run regularly from Freetown's eastern station.
- **Local Transport:** Motorcycle taxis (okada) and tricycles (kekeh) are the primary urban transport in Kenema.

## 10. Kenema: The Destination

Use this content on an "Explore Kenema" or "About Our City" page section.

Kenema is the **second most populous city in Sierra Leone** (population ~255,000) and the **capital of the Eastern Province**. It is a vibrant commercial, cultural, and administrative hub known for:

- **Diamond Mining Heritage** — Kenema is internationally recognized as a diamond mining center, with artisanal diamond fields that have shaped the region since the 1930s. Visitors can arrange guided tours of alluvial mining sites.
- **Gola Rainforest National Park** — A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and one of West Africa's most biodiverse forests. Rich in rare birds, primates, and stunning waterfalls — ideal for ecotourism, hiking, and guided nature walks.
- **Kambui Hills Forest Reserve** — Another natural escape offering scenic hiking and birdwatching close to the city.
- **Kenema Central Market** — One of Sierra Leone's most important markets, where diamonds, palm oil, cocoa, coffee, timber, and everyday goods are traded. A fascinating window into the energy of provincial commerce.
- **Mende Culture & Traditional Villages** — The Mende people are the predominant ethnic group of the Eastern Province. Nearby villages offer authentic cultural experiences including traditional dance, music, weaving, palm wine tapping, and storytelling.
- **Kpangoma Village** — A hidden gem offering immersive cultural tourism, traditional ceremonies, music, and crafts.
- **Local Cuisine** — Taste traditional Sierra Leonean dishes like cassava leaf stew, jollof rice, groundnut soup, and fresh fish at local restaurants and street food stalls throughout the city.

> *Kenema is the gateway to Sierra Leone's spectacular eastern interior — a region of lush rainforests, rich traditions, and enduring spirit.*
